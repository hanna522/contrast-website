Glaucoma

What is glaucoma?

Glaucoma is a group of eye conditions that damage the optic nerve, which is vital for good vision. This damage is often caused by abnormally high pressure in your eye. Glaucoma is one of the leading causes of blindness for people over the age of 60. However, blindness from glaucoma can often be prevented with early treatment.

How to recognize?

If you have symptoms below, go see doctor.

pen-angle glaucoma

  • gradual loss of peripheral vision
  • Tunnel vision in the advanced stages

Angle-closure glaucom

  • Severe headache
  • Eye pain and eye redness
  • Nausea and vomiting
  • Blurred vision and halos around lights

How to take care?

If you have a glaucoma, this is how to take care of yourself.

  • Medication Adherence
  • Regular Check-ups
  • Protect Your Eyes
  • Healthy Lifestyle
  • Know Your Family History
  • Avoid Activities That Increase Eye Pressure
